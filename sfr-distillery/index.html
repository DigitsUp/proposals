The Opportunity

The Gap Between Where You Are
and Where You Should Be.

SFR has real Klaviyo revenue — $10,500 last year, 22% of total. But 71% of that is manual campaigns. Every dollar from flows is automated money that works without you. Right now there are no abandoned cart flows, no post-purchase series, no winback. The infrastructure just isn't there yet. That's what this build fixes.

Missing Flow

No Abandoned Cart Recovery

Shoppers are adding bottles and leaving. Without a recovery sequence, that revenue is gone. Abandoned cart is typically the highest-ROI flow in any Klaviyo account.

Revenue Gap

Flows vs. Campaigns Imbalance

71% campaign-driven, 29% flows. Campaigns require manual effort every time. Flows run automatically. The goal is to flip that ratio over the next 90 days.

Missing Flow

No Post-Purchase Series

Someone just bought a bottle — they're the warmest customer you have. No follow-up means no second purchase, no Legendary Club invite, no tasting room upsell.

Below Benchmark

Email Attribution at 22%

Industry benchmark for a brand like SFR is 35–50% email attribution. You're at 22%. Getting to 40% on your current revenue base means an additional $8,500+ per year from email alone — before any list growth.

Quick Win

Legendary Club Email 2 Never Sends

Your second welcome email has smart sending checked with a 16-hour skip window and a 20-minute delay. It has never reached a single subscriber. One checkbox fixed in Week 1 — everyone who joins starts getting it immediately.

Missing

No UTM Tracking On Any Emails

Every email in the account is sending without UTM parameters. That means zero visibility in Google Analytics on what email is actually driving traffic and revenue. Fixed at setup — every flow and campaign properly tagged from day one.

What We're Building

Foundation First. Then Legendary.

Every brand needs the basics before the advanced stuff. We build the six foundation flows first — the ones that recover lost revenue and extend customer lifetime value. Then we layer in the four SFR-specific flows that no generic agency would think to build.

Foundation
🥃
Flow 01
Welcome Series
Trigger — New subscriber or new Legendary Club member
3–4 emails over 10 days. Introduces SFR's story, the distillery, the Legendary Club, and what Staves are. Drives the first bottle purchase before interest fades. The entry point for everything.
Foundation
🛒
Flow 02
Added to Cart
Trigger — Product added to cart, session ends without purchase
2 emails. First fires 1 hour after abandonment — product reminder, no pressure. Second fires 24 hours later with a soft nudge. No discounts needed (prohibited anyway) — the product and the story do the work.
Foundation
💳
Flow 03
Checkout Abandonment
Trigger — Checkout started, not completed
Higher purchase intent than added-to-cart. 3 emails over 48 hours. Urgency increases across the sequence. For limited releases, add scarcity language — "only X bottles left."
Foundation
👁
Flow 04
Browse Abandonment
Trigger — Viewed a product page, no add-to-cart
1–2 emails. Lighter touch. "You were looking at our 3-Year Whisky" — product photo, key callout (award-winning, small-batch, etc.), single CTA. Keeps SFR top of mind between visits.
Foundation
📦
Flow 05
Post-Purchase Series
Trigger — First purchase confirmed
4 emails over 21 days. Day 2: order confirmation + what to expect. Day 7: tasting notes + serving suggestions. Day 14: invite to the Legendary Club + Stave bonus for joining. Day 21: book a private tasting or tour. Turns a one-time buyer into a repeat customer and club member.
Foundation
🔄
Flow 06
Winback
Trigger — No purchase in 90 days
3 emails over 14 days. Chapter-aware if they're a Legendary Club member — "You're a Rule Breaker. The next drop is coming." For non-members: what's new since they bought, recent releases, tasting room events. Last email is a quiet goodbye with a clear re-entry path.
Foundation
SFR Layer
🤝
Flow 07
Referral Series (Toki)
Trigger — Member shares referral link / referred friend converts
Two branches. Branch 1 — referrer: fires when someone sends their link, confirms what they earn when the friend converts (Staves). Branch 2 — referred friend: warmer welcome, acknowledges the connection, fast-tracks to first earn action. Referral compounds — higher-chapter members are your best recruiters.
SFR Layer
⚗️
Flow 08
Points / Stave Reminder
Trigger — Member has unspent Staves, no activity in 30 days
1 email. Shows exact Stave balance, what they can redeem right now, what they're X Staves away from next. Single CTA. The goal is to pull passive earners back into the loop before they forget they're in it.
SFR Layer
🏔
Flow 09
Event & Experience Upsell
Trigger — Post-purchase (fires Day 14) + standalone segment (members who've never booked)
"You bought the bottle — now see where it was made." Private tastings, distillery tours, group bookings. For Legendary Club members: frame as a chapter-exclusive experience. This is the physical-to-digital bridge — an email that drives people to the tasting room, which in turn drives future purchases.
SFR Layer
🔥
Flow 10
Limited Release Drop Sequence
Trigger — Manual send timed to new release (triggered by tag in Klaviyo)
3-part sequence. 1 week before: "Something's coming. Legendary Club members get first access." 24 hours before: details revealed, countdown, direct CTA to reserve. Drop day: "It's here — X bottles available, Legendary Club only until [date]." SMS on drop day for Legendary members. This is what makes the club feel real.
SFR Layer
Brand Voice

Every Email Sounds Like SFR.

Copy that doesn't sound like your brand doesn't convert. Before a single email goes live, the copywriter goes through a full intake — tone, words to use, words to never use, the personas you speak to. By the end of Month 1, every email sounds like it was written by Will, Jack, or Adam.

Personas

The Rebel & The Explorer

Every piece of copy speaks to one of two personas — the rule-breaker who does things their own way, and the explorer always chasing the next experience. SFR's voice lives in the intersection.

Tone

Personal. Always.

Every email comes from a real person — Will, Jack, or Adam by name. Never a brand blast. "Whiskey has never been just about what's in the glass" — that's the energy. Warm, direct, like a message from a friend who happens to make great whiskey.

Process

Copy Intake on Kickoff

Week 1 includes a full copy intake: words you love, words you never want to see, competitors to reference, voice samples. Nothing goes live without Will's sign-off. By Day 30 the tone is locked and every future email sounds consistent.

Growth Levers

Beyond the Flows. How We Grow the List.

Flows work on the list you have. These are the levers that grow it.

Referral Program

Toki-powered. Existing members earn Staves for bringing friends in. Compounds over time — your best customers recruit for you.

Social → List Growth

Lead gen ads that drive signups directly to the Legendary Club. Paid traffic becomes owned audience.

Giveaways

Bottle drop giveaways that require email signup. Fast list growth tied to real product interest, not generic incentives.

Post-Tasting Room Trigger

Wallet Pass scan at the tasting room → follow-up email sequence. Turns in-person visits into digital relationships.

SMS for Drop Day

Nothing converts a limited release like a text. Legendary Club members get an SMS on drop day — before the email, before anyone else.
