What the segments tell us
Post-purchase is the gap
Nine of ten one-time buyers show zero activity after ordering — no opens, no visits.→ The Post-Purchase flow is the single highest-value intervention.
Email re-engages, doesn't first-convert
Abandoned-cart and checkout flows reliably convert (Marthea, Emily both did); Welcome opens run high but click-through is low.→ Optimize Welcome for clicks, not just opens.
New subscribers stall fast
The window to convert is the first 7–10 days; the 30-day cohort disengages entirely after week one.→ Front-load the Welcome offer.
Full-price is the loyalty signal
Discount usage drops sharply with loyalty; loyalists use codes rarely, mostly free-shipping thresholds.→ Stop over-discounting to acquire.
Welcome-code buyers convert worse
FIRSTWAVE15 and WSEFT33N first-order buyers aren't placing second orders — discount-driven first orders look like lower-quality customers.→ Test a low- or no-discount welcome path.
Affinity splits by gender
Men's loyalists skew shirts, shorts and the Yogger franchise; women's loyalists trend toward swimwear.→ Cross-sell by gender inside Post-Purchase.
