The one-line diagnosis

The automation works. The business is running on paid acquisition with no retention engine to catch what it brings in.

Flows actually grew 77% year over year. The revenue drop is a top-of-funnel and campaign problem, not a Klaviyo problem — and the fix is to build the bridge from first purchase to lifelong member, on the SMS-first channel that's already doing 75% of the work.

What matters most

72% / 28% first-time vs. returning

Nearly 3 in 4 recent orders are new buyers. There's no lifecycle journey connecting a first purchase to a second — the most valuable real estate in the account is empty.

75% SMS — read with one caveat

SMS earns ~9× email per recipient and is genuinely the strongest channel. But the 5-day last-click window overstates it; narrowing to 1 day gives a number you can trust. Still SMS-first either way.

$0 from two live flows

Winback fired 3 times in 30 days (wrong trigger). Soberversary earns nothing despite 45–56% SMS click rates — huge emotional engagement, zero conversion. Both are fixable fast.

Post Purchase is a stub

Two emails, no SMS, ends in days, at $0.36 per recipient — on a 75%-SMS account, at the highest-intent moment there is. This isn't a tweak; it's a rebuild, and it's the single biggest opportunity.

Campaigns are fatiguing

Revenue per send fell from ~$143 (Jan) to ~$41 (June) as frequency stayed high against a staler list. Fewer, sharper, better-segmented sends earn more per recipient.

AI search — emerging signal

An order arrived organically via ChatGPT. One in fifty — a signal, not yet a channel. Loosid is unusually well-positioned to win AI/answer-engine discovery, and that's a growth lever worth building deliberately.

The opportunity, and the first five moves

  1. Rebuild the Post Purchase flow
    Email + SMS, split by order count, category-aware cross-sell, extend to 30 days, hand off to loyalty.
    +$12–20K/yr
  2. Re-trigger Winback on a segment
    Move off the order-event trigger so the ~1,500 lapsed buyers waiting start flowing immediately.
    +$5–8K/yr
  3. Activate the dormant Smile.io loyalty flows
    5 of 6 are in draft; 600 customers hold redeemable points they've never been told about.
    +$4–8K/yr
  4. Fix & monetize Soberversary
    Turn off Smart Sending, verify date logic, build the offer around the milestone moment.
    +$3–8K/yr
  5. Grow SMS & tighten campaigns
    Opt-in form for returning non-SMS customers; clean subject lines; fewer, segmented sends.
    +$2K/1K opt-ins
✦

Bottom line for leadership

The brand and audience are working — 92% of orders are personalized milestone pieces, and email converts at the highest value of any channel. The revenue problem is structural, not creative: the lifecycle goes dark after the first purchase. Build the bridge, and the SMS engine you already have compounds instead of leaking. ~$30K+/yr is recoverable from fixes alone, before any new growth.

🟡

Read the SMS share with one caveat: the attribution window

These splits use Klaviyo's default 5-day last-click attribution — SMS gets credit for any order placed within 5 days of an SMS click, including buyers who'd have converted anyway. That overstates SMS's incremental contribution. SMS is genuinely the strongest channel here, but it's not as dominant as 75% implies.

Fix: narrow the last-click window to 1 day (start with SMS). Expect SMS's share to settle lower and email to recover some credit — which sharpens the SMS-first case rather than weakening it. Week 1

✦

The real diagnosis

In 2025, flows were ~100% email and earned $27.5K. In 2026 you added SMS, and flow revenue jumped to $48.7K — SMS now carries 77% of it. The automation strategy is working. The 50% L30D drop is driven by reduced ad spend (fewer new subscribers), a seasonal AOV dip, and campaign fatigue from over-sending a shrinking engaged audience. Fix retention and campaign discipline, and the flow engine compounds.

Campaign Strategy Review

More sends, less money: the campaign engine is fatiguing

Email campaign revenue per send has more than halved since January, and revenue per recipient dropped ~60%. You're mailing a shrinking engaged audience more often — the textbook over-send spiral.

Subject lines are working too hard

Across 126 campaigns: opens average a healthy 40.7% (MPP-inflated), but clicks sit at 1.05% and placed-order at 0.02%. The gap is the discount-everything, promo-heavy voice training people to skim.

  • 22% use promo/spam keywords; 47% contain an ALL-CAPS word; 21% emoji; 7% "!"; 4% em-dash
  • e.g. "🏆 Your February Just Got Better: 25% Off!" · "Your Path to Wellness is Just 30% Off!"

What to change

  • Remove em-dashes, "!" and promo/spam keywords — they cost deliverability and cheapen the brand.
  • Stop mailing the 3,934 never-openers on every blast (Section 4) — they drag sender reputation.
  • Segment by behavior. $0.007/recipient in June means most recipients shouldn't have gotten the send.
  • Sell the identity, not the discount. 25%-off-everything is why returning buyers wait for deals.
🟡

What changed in April?

Ad spend dropped (fewer fresh subscribers), the catalog shifted to lower-AOV summer items, and frequency stayed high against a staler list. Revenue per recipient actually peaked in April ($0.027) on the fewest sends (17) — proof that fewer, better-targeted campaigns earn more per recipient.

The loyalty paradox killing margin

Returning customers use a discount code 71% of the time vs. 58% for new (Section 6). Loyalty should reduce price sensitivity — here it's inverted. With no loyalty-only tier and the same 25%-off codes everywhere, repeat buyers are trained to wait for a deal. Activating the Smile flows + a members-only reward tier replaces blanket discounts with earned ones — protecting margin while lifting repeat rate.

Revenue opportunity: nudging even 10% of the 497 reward-eligible to redeem-and-buy at ~$45 AOV ≈ $2,250 immediately, with recurring lift as the points loop runs on every order — plus a referral engine to layer on top.

One principle behind every persona recommendation

Loosid's catalog spans far more than apparel — tees, hoodies, hats, jewelry, sobriety coins, wall art, household goods, and Soberversary gifts. So we anchor the strategy to what's durable: the personalized milestone identity. The journey shape stays constant; the specific products flex with the season and the customer. That's why the cross-sell below is built category-agnostic.

Persona 1 · 72%

The Milestone Buyer new customers

  • Discovers Loosid via a META/Instagram paid ad
  • First purchase is a personalized milestone piece — whatever's converting that season (tee, hat, jewelry, coin)
  • Driven by a specific milestone — 1 year, 2 years, an anniversary
  • Uses an acquisition code (WELCOME25, EXTRA25); opts into SMS at checkout
  • $49.37 AOV · 1.3 items. Won't return without a deliberate touchpoint
Persona 2 · 28%

The Community Repeat Buyer returning

  • Owns an entry piece; returns to expand the identity across categories — apparel, accessories, home, art
  • Converts at the highest value via Klaviyo email ($65.82)
  • Still buys personalized items 86% of the time — the connection stays
  • More discount-dependent (71% use a code) — needs a reason, not a deal
  • $44.41 AOV. Responds to loyalty, new drops, the Soberversary moment

The retention ladder (this is the post-purchase spine — category-agnostic)

The journey isn't "hat → tee." It's milestone piece → complete the identity → live it → display & gift it. The rebuilt Post Purchase flow walks each buyer up this ladder based on what they actually bought and the season they came in through.

RUNG 1 · ENTRY

The milestone piece

Personalized sober-date tee, hat, jewelry, or coin. The emotional first purchase.

RUNG 2 · COMPLETE

The look

Apparel + accessories that round out the identity — hoodie, second tee, dog tag, more.

RUNG 3 · LIVE IT

The lifestyle

Household: mugs, candles, blankets, journals, phone cases — sobriety at home.

RUNG 4 · DISPLAY & GIFT

Art & gifting

Wall art & canvases (highest AOV, $45–80) + coins, cards, party favors for their milestones and others'.

🔴

The missing bridge — where 60%+ of the retention opportunity sits

There's no lifecycle journey connecting Persona 1 to Persona 2. Post-purchase runs at $0.36 RPR, email-only. No loyalty onboarding. No "what's next." No referral ask. The gap between first and second purchase is empty — and it's the most valuable real estate in the account.

Emerging personas worth validating

The full catalog points to buyers beyond the core two — each a distinct growth opportunity to develop:

  • The Gifter — buying for a sober loved one. Lives in the Soberversary world: coins, jewelry, greeting cards, party favors. Occasion-driven, gift-coded, higher AOV with seasonal spikes.
  • The Facility / B2B buyer — "Treatment Center Wall Art" and bulk coins point to sober-living homes and treatment centers buying in volume. A high-AOV, repeatable account channel.
  • The Community member — 450k+ app members (Loosid community + dating). An owned audience and acquisition/loyalty lever far larger than the email list.
✦

Emerging channel: AI search (your unfair advantage)

One of the 50 orders arrived organically via ChatGPT. That's 1-in-50 — a signal, not yet a channel — but it appeared with zero effort, which is the tell. Loosid is unusually well-positioned to win AI/answer-engine discovery: it's a category-defining brand for high-intent queries ("personalized sober date shirt," "sobriety anniversary gift," "recovery wall art"), with a 450k+ community, editorial/blog content, and real press (USA Today, Yahoo, Elite Daily) — exactly the signals LLMs lean on when recommending products.

The play: treat AI search (AEO) as a deliberate growth channel — structure product and content for how answer engines cite and recommend brands. It's a forward-looking expansion area DigitsUp can build with you (see Proposal).
