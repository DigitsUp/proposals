Five Findings That Matter
The fastest paths to more revenue.
Each one is confirmed, scoped, and fixable.
01
There's a winback opportunity waiting for every lapsed customer.
The Purchaser Winback flow has delivered zero emails and generated zero revenue — not because it's broken, but because the trigger requires a 100-day delay calculated from order date. No existing customer qualifies. Based on 20 real customer journey profiles pulled for this audit, 90 days is the right activation point — that's when 44% of the repurchase window opens. Converting to a segment-based trigger starts reaching lapsed customers within days.
02
60% of Frame Fanatics loyalists have earned points they've never seen.
Loyalists in this program average 12+ purchases. 60% have never formally enrolled in Frame Fanatics — not because they opted out, but because the program launched in January 2025 and pre-existing customers were never brought in. Yotpo has silently assigned them tier status based on their purchase history. The points and the tier are there. The introduction hasn't happened yet. One re-enrollment campaign reaches 45K+ pre-2025 buyers.
03
Smarter sends — not more sends — is the path to campaign growth.
eyebobs already sends email 5+ days per week. Revenue per recipient has held flat year-over-year at $0.047 — the quality of sends hasn't declined. The H1 gap comes from fewer total recipients reached. The opportunity is in segmentation: VIP and loyalty-aware sends in this account are returning $0.25–$0.58 per recipient vs. $0.04–$0.08 for broad sends — a 5 to 10x difference within the same program.
04
New subscribers deserve a better first week.
New subscribers enter a program with no coordination between the welcome flow and live campaigns. In the 10 new customer journeys pulled for this audit, the median subscriber received 25 emails before their first purchase — with welcome flow and campaign sends running simultaneously and up to three different discount offers in the same window. When abandon flows fired, they closed the journey. When they didn't, a broad discount campaign did — almost always at margin cost. The fix is a 10-day campaign suppression window for new subscribers and one consistent offer across all entry flows.
05
10,811 customers have reward points they've never been told about.
The Points Reminder flow generated $46 in the first half of 2026. Smart sending is suppressing 99% of sends to a list of 10,811 profiles — each holding $5 to $20 in redeemable points they don't know about. This is a high-intent email going to customers who want to hear from the brand. Removing smart sending, switching to a segment trigger by points balance, and adding an SMS layer turns one of the lowest-performing flows in the account into one of the highest.

The RX Renewal flow is already live and generating revenue. A prescription renewal reminder series — built around that signal — is a customer-first touchpoint with no equivalent in the current program.
